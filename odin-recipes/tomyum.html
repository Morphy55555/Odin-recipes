Tom Yum Soup Recipe

Description

Tom Yum Soup is a famous Thai soup known for its bold, spicy, and sour flavors.

The broth is made from fresh herbs like lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal, combined with chili peppers and fish sauce for a unique taste. Tom Yum is often served with shrimp (Tom Yum Goong) or chicken (Tom Yum Gai), and can also include mushrooms, tomatoes, and fresh herbs. It's a flavorful, tangy dish that's both refreshing and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp or Chicken
  • Lemongrass
  • Galangal
  • Chili Peppers
  • Kaffir Lime Leaves
  • Fish Sauce
  • Lime Juice
  • Mushrooms
  • Tomatoes
  • Thai Birdâ€™s Eye Chili

Instructions

  1. Bring water or broth to a boil in a large pot, and add lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and chili peppers to infuse the flavor.
  2. Once the broth is aromatic, add shrimp (or chicken) and cook until done.
  3. Add mushrooms and tomatoes to the soup and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in fish sauce and lime juice to taste. Adjust seasoning to your preferred level of saltiness or sourness.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with extra lime wedges and chili for heat.
