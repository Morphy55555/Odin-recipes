Green Curry Recipe

Description

Green Curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish that combines the bold spices and herbs typical of Thai cuisine.

Curry is made with a fragrant green curry paste, which is traditionally prepared by blending fresh ingredients such as green chilies, garlic, ginger, lemongrass, and Thai basil. These ingredients give the curry its signature bright, slightly spicy, and herbal flavor.

Curry is typically cooked with coconut milk, creating a rich and creamy sauce that complements the heat of the curry paste. Vegetables, tofu, or meat (such as chicken, beef, or shrimp) are often added, along with bamboo shoots, bell peppers, and Thai eggplants. The dish is typically served with jasmine rice to balance out the spiciness. Garnished with fresh cilantro or Thai basil, Thai Green Curry is a perfect blend of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy flavors that make it a beloved dish both in Thailand and worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Green Curry Paste
  • Coconut Milk
  • Beef, or Tofu
  • Bamboo Shoots
  • Bell Peppers
  • Thai Eggplantsli
  • Kaffir Lime Leave
  • Vegetable Oil

Instructions

  1. Start by cooking the jasmine rice according to package instructions. Set aside when done.
  2. In a large saucepan or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the green curry paste to the pan and fry for about 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently, until fragrant.
  4. Slowly pour in coconut milk while stirring, mixing the curry paste into the milk. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Add the chicken (or tofu/vegetables) to the curry and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through (or tofu is lightly browned).
  6. Stir in the bamboo shoots, red bell pepper, and Thai eggplants (if using). Continue simmering for another 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Add fish sauce (or soy sauce) and sugar to balance the flavors,If you like extra heat, you can add sliced bird's eye chilies at this point.
  8. Tear the kaffir lime leaves into pieces and add them to the curry.
  9. Stir in the Thai basil leaves and cook for another 1-2 minutes, just until the herbs wilt and release their aroma.
  10. Taste the curry and adjust seasoning if necessary. Add more fish sauce, sugar, or lime juice to balance the flavors.
  11. Serve: Spoon the curry over the cooked jasmine rice. Garnish with additional fresh basil leaves or sliced chilies if desired.
