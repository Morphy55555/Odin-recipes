Nasi Goreng Recipe

Description

Nasi Goreng is Indonesia's iconic fried rice dish, often served with a fried egg on top and a variety of meats or vegetables.

The rice is stir-fried with sweet soy sauce (kecap manis), garlic, shallots, and chilies for a savory, umami-packed flavor. Common ingredients include shrimp, chicken, and vegetables like peas and carrots. Nasi Goreng is a versatile dish that can be served as a quick meal or as a celebratory feast in Indonesia.

Ingredients

  • Cooked Rice (preferably cold)
  • Shrimp, Chicken, or Tofu
  • Shallots
  • Garlic
  • Kecap Manis (Sweet Soy Sauce)
  • Eggs
  • Green Onions
  • Carrots
  • Peas
  • Chili Peppers

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Fry the shrimp (or chicken/tofu) until cooked through and set aside.
  3. Add chopped shallots, garlic, and chilies to the pan and saut√© until fragrant.
  4. Add the cold, cooked rice to the pan and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Stir in kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), then add carrots and peas. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  6. Push the rice mixture to one side, crack an egg into the pan, and scramble. Mix it into the rice once cooked.
  7. Serve the fried rice topped with a fried egg, sliced cucumber, and fresh cilantro for garnish.
