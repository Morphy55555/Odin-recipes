Pad Thai Recipe

Description

Pad Thai is a popular Thai stir-fried noodle dish made with rice noodles, eggs, and a combination of ingredients like shrimp, tofu, peanuts, and bean sprouts.

The dish is known for its unique balance of flavors: sweet, sour, salty, and spicy. The noodles are stir-fried with tamarind paste, fish sauce, and sugar, and then garnished with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and fresh herbs. Pad Thai is often served as a street food in Thailand and is beloved by people all over the world.

Ingredients

  • Rice Noodles
  • Shrimp or Tofu
  • Eggs
  • Garlic
  • Tamarind Paste
  • Fish Sauce
  • Sugar
  • Crushed Peanuts
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Lime Wedges

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for 20-30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil in a pan or wok over medium heat, and cook shrimp (or tofu) until fully cooked.
  3. Add minced garlic to the pan and stir-fry for about 30 seconds.
  4. Push the shrimp (or tofu) and garlic to the side, and scramble the eggs in the same pan.
  5. Add the soaked noodles to the pan, and stir-fry with tamarind paste, fish sauce, and sugar for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Stir in bean sprouts and cook for another minute.
  7. Serve the Pad Thai garnished with crushed peanuts, fresh cilantro, lime wedges, and chili flakes for extra heat.
